Tax Slabs & Rates

Both regimes remain available for individuals. IT Act 2025 renumbers sections but rates are unchanged. New regime is the default from TY 2026-27.

✓ Budget 2026 — No Changes to Tax SlabsUnion Budget 2026 (February 2026) confirmed NO changes to income tax slabs, rebate limits, or standard deduction for FY 2026-27. The enhanced rebate of ₹60,000 (zero tax up to ₹12 lakh under new regime) continues unchanged. IT Act 2025 is revenue-neutral — same rates, just renumbered sections.

IT Act 1961 → IT Act 2025 — Section Mapping

From 1 April 2026, IT Act 2025 replaces IT Act 1961 for new transactions. The new tax regime moves from Sec 115BAC (1961) → Sec 202 (2025). The rebate under Sec 87A (1961) → Sec 159 (2025). Tax rates are unchanged.

Salary Income

Income taxable under the head "Salaries". Sections are renumbered but provisions are identical under IT Act 2025.

IT Act 1961 → IT Act 2025 — Salary Section Mapping

The substantive provisions for salary income are retained but renumbered in IT Act 2025.

HRA Exemption Sec 10(13A) / 1961→Sec 16(3) / 2025

Exemption = Least of the following 3: 1. Actual HRA received from employer
2. 50% of basic salary + DA (metro) or 40% (non-metro)
3. Rent paid − 10% of basic salary + DA

Metro cities: Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai. If rent > ₹1 lakh/year — landlord PAN mandatory.
Note: HRA exemption is NOT available under the New Tax Regime.

Leave Travel Allowance (LTA) Sec 10(5) / 1961→Sec 16(8) / 2025

Conditions for exemption: Covers actual travel cost for employee + family. Air: economy class fare. Rail/road: first-class AC fare. Claimable 2 times in a 4-year block. Current block: 2026–2029 (effective 1 January 2026). Previous block 2022–2025 ended. Not available under New Regime.

House Property Income

Income from let-out or deemed let-out property taxed under "Income from House Property". Sections largely retained with minor renumbering.

IT Act 1961 → IT Act 2025 — House Property Section Mapping

Sec 22 (Basis of charge) → Sec 22 Sec 23 (Annual value) → Sec 23 Sec 24 (Deductions) → Sec 25 Sec 26 (Co-owners) → Sec 26 Effective: 1 April 2026

🏠 Self-Occupied Property (SOP) Sec 23(2) / 1961→Sec 23(2) / 2025

  • Annual value = Nil
  • Max 2 properties treated as SOP
  • Home loan interest deduction — up to ₹2,00,000 per year (Old regime only)
  • New regime — NO interest deduction on SOP
  • Pre-construction interest: 5 equal instalments from year of completion

🏢 Let-Out Property Sec 23(1) / 1961→Sec 23(1) / 2025

  • Annual value = Gross Annual Value − Municipal taxes
  • Standard deduction = 30% of Net Annual Value Sec 24(a) → Sec 25(a)
  • Home loan interest — full deduction (no limit) Sec 24(b) → Sec 25(b)
  • Municipal tax paid — fully deductible
  • Loss set-off against other heads — up to ₹2 lakh

Set-Off and Carry Forward of Loss Sec 71 / 1961→Sec 96 / 2025

Loss TypeSet-Off (same year)Carry Forward Period
HP loss — Old RegimeSet off against any income — max ₹2 lakh in same yearBalance carried forward 8 years — only vs HP income
HP loss — New Regime❌ Cannot set off against any other income headCarried forward 8 years — only vs future HP income
HP loss exceeding ₹2 lakh (Old Regime)Not set off in same year8 years — only vs HP income
⚠ New Regime RestrictionUnder new tax regime: No deduction for home loan interest on self-occupied property. House property loss CANNOT be set off against other income — only carried forward against future HP income.

Tax on profit from sale of capital assets — property, shares, mutual funds, gold, bonds. Revised by Finance Bill 2024 (effective 23 July 2024).

Advance Tax

Pay tax in instalments during the financial year when total tax liability exceeds ₹10,000 after TDS. Identical under both Acts — only section numbers change.

IT Act 1961 → IT Act 2025 — Advance Tax Section Mapping

Sec 207 (Liability) → Sec 249 Sec 208 (Conditions) → Sec 250 Sec 211 (Instalments) → Sec 253 Sec 234B (Default) → Sec 274 Sec 234C (Deferment) → Sec 275 Effective: 1 April 2026

Instalment Schedule Sec 211 / 1961→Sec 253 / 2025

1st15 June15%—

Final15 March100%100% by 15 March

Sec 234B / Sec 274 (2025) — Default

If advance tax paid < 90% of assessed tax — interest at 1% per month from 1 April of AY till actual payment date.

Sec 234C / Sec 275 (2025) — Deferment

If each instalment is short — interest at 1% per month for 3 months (1 month for last instalment) on the shortfall.

Safe harbourIf advance tax paid ≥ 90% of total tax — no interest u/s 234B / Sec 274. Salaried employees: if employer deducts TDS on full salary, advance tax obligation is usually covered automatically.
